Beef in Black Bean Sauce

A delicious korean dish.

Bibimbap is a traditional Korean dish that offers a delightful blend of flavors and textures. It consists of a bowl of warm white rice topped with an array of seasoned vegetables, such as carrots, spinach, bean sprouts, and mushrooms. Thinly sliced meat, usually beef, is often added, along with a fried egg. The dish is completed with a dollop of gochujang (Korean chili paste) for a spicy kick, and a sprinkle of sesame seeds for added flavor. Before eating, all the ingredients are mixed together, creating a harmonious and vibrant meal that is both nutritious and satisfying.

Ingredients

  • 100g thin beef steak
  • 2 tsp light soy sauce, plus extra to serve
  • 120g rice
  • Sunflower oil
  • 1 carrot, cut into matchsticks
  • 50g spinach
  • 2 eggs
  • Â½ tsp toasted sesame seeds
  • Thumb sized piece of fresh root ginger, peeled and cut into fine matchsticks
  • Sauce:
  • 2 tbsp gochujang or 4 tsp sriracha and 2 tsp white miso paste
  • 2 tsp toasted sesame seeds
  • 2 tsp cider vinegar
  • 4 tsp light soy sauce

Steps

  1. Marinate the steak in soy sauce.
  2. Cook the rice according to package instructions. Heat 1 tsp oil in a pan or wok, fry the steak until browned, then rest under foil. In the same pan, stir-fry the carrots for 2-3 mins, transfer to a plate. Fry the spinach until wilted, then fry the eggs, adding more oil if needed.
  3. Drain and divide the rice into 2 bowls. Slice the steak and place on the rice. Add the carrots, spinach, and ginger. Sprinkle sesame seeds on top. Mix all sauce ingredients and serve alongside. To eat, add sauce, break the egg, and mix everything together.
